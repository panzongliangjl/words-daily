1

apologize

/əˈpɒlədʒaɪz/ 英   /əˈpɑːlədʒaɪz/ 美 verb
Oxford Definition

To say that you are sorry for doing something wrong or causing a problem. 因做错事或引发问题而表示歉意。

"I sincerely apologize for the delay."

我为延误诚挚道歉。

"He apologized profusely to his family."

他向家人连连道歉。

Grammar & Usage

Usually used as an intransitive verb (不及物动词). Common patterns:

apologize to sb apologize for sth apologize to sb for sth
Synonyms
say sorry express regret make an apology
词源 Etymology: From Greek apologizesthai (to defend oneself), originally meaning "to speak in one's own defense," later evolving to express regret.
记忆小提示
联想 "a + policy（政策）+ ize" — 公司出了问题要出台"政策"来处理，就是在正式道歉。核心词根 apolog- 来自希腊语，意为"为自己辩护/解释"。
2

grateful

/ˈɡreɪtfl/ adjective
Oxford Definition

1. Feeling or showing thanks because somebody has done something kind for you or has done as you asked. 因某人为你做了好事或应你所求而表示感谢的。

"We are very grateful to all those who contributed."

我们非常感谢所有提供帮助的人。

2. Used to make a request, especially in a letter or in a formal situation. 用于正式场合或书信中表示礼貌请求。

"I would be grateful if you could send the completed form back as soon as possible."

如您能尽快将填好的表格寄回，我将不胜感激。

Common Patterns
grateful to sb grateful for sth grateful to sb for sth grateful that...
Word Family
gratefully (adv.) gratitude (n.) ungrateful (adj. 反义词)
词源 Etymology: Mid 16th century, from obsolete grate (pleasing, thankful, from Latin gratus) + -ful.
记忆小提示
grate（感激）+ -ful（充满）= 内心充满感激。也可以联想 "Great + ful" — 觉得对方做的事情很 "great"，所以你心存感激！
3

hesitate

/ˈhezɪteɪt/ verb
Oxford Definition

1. To be slow to speak or act because you feel uncertain or nervous. 因不确定或紧张而说话或行动迟缓。

"She hesitated before replying."

她回答前犹豫了一下。

2. To be worried about doing something, especially because you are not sure that it is right or appropriate. 担心做某事，尤因不确定是否恰当。

"Please do not hesitate to contact me."

请随时联系我（不要犹豫）。

Common Patterns
hesitate to do sth hesitate about/over sth hesitate between A and B
Word Family
hesitant (adj.) hesitantly (adv.) hesitation (n.)
Proverb

"He who hesitates (is lost)."

犹豫者败；机不可失，时不再来。

词源 Etymology: Early 17th century, from Latin haesitat- (stuck, undecided), from haesitare (to stick, remain fixed).
记忆小提示
谐音记忆："黑死他" — 想起某人令你犹豫不定，就像被"黑死"在原地，动弹不得。或者联想词根 hesit- = stick（粘住），被粘住了就无法前进，只能犹豫。
4

overwhelmed

/ˌoʊvəˈwelmd/ adjective
Oxford Definition (from overwhelm)

1. To have such a strong emotional effect on somebody that it is difficult for them to resist or know how to react. （情绪上）使难以承受；使不知所措。

"She was overwhelmed by feelings of guilt."

她被内疚感淹没了。

"The beauty of the landscape overwhelmed me."

风景之美让我震撼不已。

2. To defeat somebody completely. 彻底击败；压倒。

"The army was overwhelmed by the rebels."

军队被叛军彻底击溃。

3. To be given too much of something; to have too many things to deal with. （因数量或强度过大）使应接不暇；使负担过重。

"We were overwhelmed by requests for information."

信息请求让我们应接不暇。

Common Patterns
be overwhelmed by sth be overwhelmed with sth feel overwhelmed
Word Family
overwhelm (v.) overwhelming (adj.) overwhelmingly (adv.)
Synonyms
overcome (情感上) overpower (击败) flood (淹没)
词源 Etymology: From over (超过) + whelm (古英语，意为"覆盖、淹没"). Originally meant to turn something upside down or cover completely.
记忆小提示
over（超过）+ whelm（淹没）= 被超量淹没了。想象海浪 over 你，把你完全 whelm（淹）掉，自然就不知所措了。这个词完美描述了现代人面对工作压力时的状态！
5

compromise

/ˈkɒmprəmaɪz/ 英   /ˈkɑːmprəmaɪz/ 美 noun / verb
Oxford Definition (Noun)

1. An agreement made between two people or groups in which each side gives up some of the things they want so that both sides are happy at the end. 妥协；和解协议。

"After lengthy talks, the two sides finally reached a compromise."

经过漫长的谈判，双方最终达成了妥协。

2. A solution to a problem in which two or more things cannot exist together as they are, in which each thing is reduced or changed slightly so that they can exist together. （对立观点或需求间的）折中方案。

"This model represents the best compromise between price and quality."

这款型号代表了价格与质量之间的最佳折中。

Oxford Definition (Verb)

1. To give up some of your demands after a disagreement with somebody, in order to reach an agreement. 妥协，让步。

"Neither side is prepared to compromise."

双方都不愿妥协。

2. To do something that is against your principles or does not reach standards that you have set. 违背原则，降低标准。

"I refuse to compromise my principles."

我拒绝违背自己的原则。

Common Patterns
reach/arrive at a compromise compromise on sth compromise between A and B
Synonyms
agreement concession settlement
词源 Etymology: From Middle English, via Old French compromis and Latin compromissum (mutual promise to abide by arbitration), from com- (together) + promittere (to promise).
记忆小提示
com（共同）+ promise（承诺）= 双方各退一步、共同做出承诺 = 妥协。生活中很多事，不是非赢即输，而是找到 compromise。记住：妥协不是失败，而是智慧！
